Legal Framework and Basis for Participation and Earnings in Commission Sharing Programs in the United States

1. Freedom of Contract and Association:

The United States upholds the fundamental principles of freedom of contract and association. Individuals have the right to engage in voluntary agreements and associations, provided they do not violate the law. Affiliate marketing and commission-sharing programs are examples of voluntary agreements between parties.

2. Commercial Freedom of Speech:

The First Amendment of the United States Constitution protects freedom of speech, extending to commercial expression. Participating in affiliate marketing programs involves promoting products or services, a form of commercial expression generally considered protected by the First Amendment.

3. Antitrust and Competition Laws:

Antitrust laws, such as the Sherman Act and the Clayton Act, aim to promote competition and prevent anticompetitive business practices. Affiliate marketing programs, by allowing diverse individuals to participate in promoting and selling products, and foster competition and diversity in the marketplace.

4. Property Rights and Voluntary Contracts:

Property rights are fundamental in the U.S. legal system. Commission-sharing programs operate within the framework of voluntary contracts, where participants agree to the terms and conditions of their involvement. Respect for property rights and freedom of contract support these types of agreements.

5. E-Commerce Regulation:

Laws and regulations related to e-commerce support the validity of online transactions, including those related to affiliate marketing programs. These laws establish the legal framework for online commercial transactions and provide protections for participants.
